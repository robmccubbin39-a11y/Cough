Post-Infective Cough

Coughing for weeks after a cold is frustrating, but usually a sign of your body’s healing process. Explore the clinical data on recovery and relief.

Expected Duration: 3–8 Weeks

Understanding the "Long Tail"

A viral infection can strip away the protective lining of your airways. This leaves underlying cough receptors exposed and "twitchy," leading to spasms triggered by cold air or even talking.

Phase 1

Inflammation peaks as the virus clears.

Phase 2

Hypersensitivity lasts 2-4 weeks.

Inflammation Recovery Timeline

The Antibiotic Reality

Over **90% of acute coughs** are viral. Antibiotics do not work on viruses and do not speed up the healing of sensitized airways.

Coloured mucus is not a definitive sign of bacterial infection.

Avoid resistance: Unnecessary antibiotics can cause stomach issues and allergic reactions.

Managing Your Symptoms

While time is the only cure for the airway lining, these evidence-based interventions can provide significant relief from the daily "tickle."

Natural Honey

A simple spoonful of honey (for adults and children over 12 months) effectively coats the throat and calms receptors.

Saline Irrigation

Clearing post-nasal drip using saline sprays or washes is critical for stopping the "night-time" cough reflex.

Hydration

Constant small sips of water keep mucous membranes hydrated and physically break the cycle of a tickling spasm.

When to see Dr. Rob

Some symptoms indicate a more serious condition requiring physical assessment.

URGENT REVIEW NEEDED

8-Week Mark

Any cough that persists beyond 8 weeks in adults or 4 weeks in children.

Blood Presence

Any traces of blood or dark brown/rusty phlegm in your cough.

Breathing Issues

New wheezing, shortness of breath, or sharp pains in the chest.

Systemic Fever

A return of high fever, night sweats, or unexplained weight loss.

Voice Changes

Persistent hoarseness or difficulty swallowing food and fluids.

Health History

Patients with a long history of smoking or underlying COPD/Asthma.
